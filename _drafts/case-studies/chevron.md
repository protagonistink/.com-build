## SOURCE CONTENT (from pkthewriter)

**Context (headline):**
It's the 21st Century. Energy is evolving. One of the world's biggest oil companies was ready to evolve with it.

**The Conflict:**
But evolution for a 150-year-old energy company isn't just about technology. It's about trust.

Chevron operates in an industry that's highly technical, extremely scientific, and heavily regulated. The wrong content strategy can undermine credibility. Even the wrong word can confuse one audience and alienate another. Their existing language was dense, insider-driven, and hard to read for anyone outside the industry.

Not to mention — 90% of websites are now read on their tablets or phones. Scrollable was the name of the game.

This wasn't just simplifying the copy. It was making a giant relevant to today's humans.

**The Resolution:**
We rebuilt Chevron.com from the ground up. The goal was to bring humanity to a major player in a highly technical, highly scientific, and highly regulated industry. The copy drove the redesign, from content strategy to storytelling to choosing the words that needed focus. We traded the existing language for a fresh voice and a clear strategy at an easy-to-read level for modern device-scrolling audiences.

**Editorial Section — "Renewable Fuels on the Road":**
This hub-and-spoke experience breaks down the future of fuels for every transportation category. We covered general education and took deeper looks into each category. The result is a multi-page experience and one of the highest-trafficked pages on chevron.com.
*(Included images: Trucking, Rail, Shipping screenshots. Vimeo: https://vimeo.com/926419977)*

**Disciplines:** Content Strategy · Copywriting · Voice & Tone Discovery · Team Management
**Deliverables:** Brand Story · Content Strategy · Modernized Brand Voice · UX and Website Copy

**Impact:**
- The Renewable Fuels hub became one of the highest-trafficked destinations on Chevron.com.
- Established a new brand voice that can scale across the site.
- Made the .com function as a human-first narrative system, instead of a simple brochure.
