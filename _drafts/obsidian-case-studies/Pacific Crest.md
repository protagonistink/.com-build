# Pacific Crest

> [!note]
> This one should be written as a workshop case study, not stretched into a broader engagement if the workshop is the real center of gravity.

## What We Know

- Pacific Crest should be framed as a workshop case study.
- The story should focus on setup, intervention, outputs, and the shift that followed.

## Drafting Goal

Write a compact, concrete case study that shows the workshop as a serious intervention: why it was needed, what happened in the room, what came out of it, and what changed after.

## Facts To Gather

- What kind of organization Pacific Crest is
- Why the workshop was needed
- Who was in the room
- How long the workshop was
- What decisions or language came out of it
- What changed afterward

## Possible Section Shape

### Prologue

- The pre-workshop problem
- Why alignment could not wait

### Showcase — Before the Room

- Conflicting language
- Strategic drift
- Decision bottlenecks

### Showcase — The Workshop

- How the workshop was structured
- What pressure it put on the team
- What it forced into the open

### Optional Deliverables

- Core story spine
- Messaging decisions
- Prioritized next-step language

### Climax — The Shift

- What the team left with
- What became easier or clearer afterward

## Questions To Answer Before Writing

- What was the exact workshop brief?
- Was the output primarily narrative, strategic, verbal, or operational?
- What evidence exists that the workshop changed anything?
- Is there a clean artifact set to show, or is this mostly a writing case?

## Asset / Evidence Needs

- Workshop agenda or board capture
- Any output doc safe to show
- One image or visual anchor if the page needs it

## Public-Tone Guardrails

- Keep it about the intervention, not generic process talk
- Do not oversell the result if the proof is modest
- Let the workshop format be the point
